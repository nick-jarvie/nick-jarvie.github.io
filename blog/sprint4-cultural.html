Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

and how it influences your learning


Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principals of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to form new connections and pathways throughout a person’s life, more specificity it is when the neurons and neural networks of your brain change their connections as a result of new information and stimulation. Repeated stimulation of the same networks helps to strengthen the pathway. So the more a person performs a task the easier they will be able to recall or perform that activity in the future.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

The more I practice an activity, in this case coding, the easier it should be to perform in the future. Repeating an activity, retrieving a memory, and reviewing material in a variety of ways helps to build thicker, stronger, more hard-wired connections. It also helps to put information into context and prioritise activities that help to tap into already-existing pathways.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

I think some of the best ways to increase my own neuroplasticity is to engage with a lot of the methods we have touched on already throughout the sprints. Such as having a defined and consistent schedule and getting enough sleep. Setting clear and meaningful goals, taking small and important steps in learning and adopting a growth mindset. Practicing mindfulness, exercising and raising your standards and to stop procrastinating.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

A mindset is the way we approach and deal with a particular situation.
A Growth Mindset is understanding that skills and abilities can be improved through persistence and work rather than being a fixed trait.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

That you can be both mindsets and often switch between them. I think I particularly related to this point as in the past there have definitely been times when I have been less confident and thus less inclined to learn but others when I have been the opposite.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I think proving to myself in small steps that I’m capable of developing both my skillset and mindset will be very important as I continue working through the material, most of which is new to me. So I think maintaining the “not yet” attitude as I encounter any problems and embracing the challenge and difficulty will be an important aspect in my learning journey.
