What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is someone’s ability to identify emotions. It is how well people are able to evaluate and perceive how someone else feels. While also being a measure of how well they control and express their own emotions. It is also how they use their emotions to relate to others and interact in social settings.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) is traditionally used to determine ones academic ability and is usually derived from a standardized test to assess intelligence. IQ is often indicative of someone’s ability to solve a challenging task, analyse, reason and recall information.
Whereas someone’s emotional intelligence is more their ‘soft’ skills or their ability to identify, assess and control their own and others emotions alone and in a group.

Why is it important?

Someone’s emotional intelligence can often be a measure of how well they look after their own physical and mental health. Their ability to manage effective relationships and even their ability to inspire and lead. It can be a measure for ones capacity to motivate, communicate and be empathic to others. In some ways it can be an indication of ones well-being, fulfilment and overall happiness.
