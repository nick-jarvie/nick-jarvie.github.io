Margin, Border, and Padding


What is the difference between
Margin, Border, and Padding?

Margin

The margin is the defined extra space around an element, outside the border.
The margin property can be specified by four values; top, bottom, left and right.
The size of these values is determined by a fixed <length> value, a <percentage> relative to the width of the containing block or auto, were the browser selects a suitable margin to use.

Border

The border is the line between the margin and padding of the content and will be invisible unless its style is defined.
You can style and define the border by giving values to width, border-style, and the border-color properties.

Padding

The padding is the space on the inside of your element,
between the content and border.
It effects how your object is positioned within the border.
The padding can be specified using four values; top, bottom, left and right.
Each value is determined by a fixed <length> or a <percentage>
